## Growth Operations Framework (7 steps)

Apply this framework when diagnosing or building a growth operation from scratch.

**1. Align on the real business problem**
Growth starts by defining the actual problem and the constraints around it. Don't optimize the wrong thing.

**2. Nail your North Star metric**
Long-term profitability → 12-18 month LTV:CAC as North Star. Acquisition-stage → activation rate or qualified pipeline. Pick one metric that reflects real value creation, not vanity.

**3. Get your data foundation right**
10 tools, 10 teams, 10 definitions of "customer" = chaos. Align on a single source of truth before running any experiment. If data is dirty, fix that first.

**4. Strengthen the feedback loop**
Attribution isn't just hard — attribution alone isn't enough. Build feedback loops that capture qualitative signal (sales calls, churn interviews, user behavior) alongside attribution data to understand *why* things work.

**5. Build cross-functional accountability**
Marketing finds ways to scale within constraints — but only when product, sales, and CS are aligned on the same metrics and timelines. Define who owns what at each funnel stage.

**6. Optimize for capital efficiency & scale**
Not just "what works" — what works best, and is it scalable and replicable? A channel that converts at 5x but caps at $10K/month isn't a growth engine. Prioritize channels with scalable unit economics.

**7. Execute fast. Iterate faster.**
Speed beats perfection. Tight loops win. Ship experiments in days, not months. Kill losers fast. Double down on winners immediately.

## GTM Framework (3 stages)

**1. Market Needs**
Select a segment and validate customer needs qualitatively (interviews, patterns) and quantitatively (size, frequency, willingness to pay).

**2. Product Strategy**
Define positioning, build competitive advantage, and design product strategy: what to build, for whom, and why it wins.

**3. Go To Market**
Design the acquisition strategy, distribution channels, and monetization model to reach the market effectively and scalably.

## 3 Fundamentals of GTM Strategy

A great GTM is the intersection of three elements. Missing any one breaks the whole system.

```
        ICP
       /    \
Willingness   Value
  to Pay     Narrative
     \          /
  Pricing & ── Positioning &
  Packaging    Messaging
       \      /
     Perceived
       Value
         ↓
   Compelling Offer
```

- **ICP + Positioning & Messaging** → Value Narrative: *Right ICP + right story = your message actually lands.*
- **Positioning & Messaging + Pricing & Packaging** → Perceived Value: *Right story + right pricing = people buy the outcome, not the feature.*
- **ICP + Pricing & Packaging** → Willingness to Pay: *Right ICP + right pricing = you sell the way they want to buy.*

Apply this as a diagnostic when GTM isn't working: which intersection is broken?

## TAM Activation Framework (B2B SaaS)

*Source: Douwe Wester — Ideal Customer-Led GTM*

Activating your TAM requires three sequential steps: detect the trigger, activate across channels calibrated to warmth, and measure intent signals.

---

### Step 1 · Triggers — The "Why Now"

People don't move without a reason. Detect it first.

**Business Triggers** *(something changed inside the company)*
- M&A → new entity, new stack needed
- Funding → budget just unlocked
- Leadership hire → CRO, VP Sales, CMO
- Reorg → priorities just shifted

**Demand Triggers** *(observable signals of active search)*
- Job postings → roles in your category
- Stack changes → added/dropped a competitor
- Website updates → new page, new angle
- G2 / review activity → shortlisting in public

> Rule: Never open cold outreach without referencing a trigger. The trigger IS the opener.

---

### Step 2 · Activate — 4 Channels on a Warmth Axis

Tactics shift as accounts warm up. Run all 4 channels in parallel, but prioritize the right tactic per account temperature.

```
COLD ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────► WARM
```

#### Content (Build trust)

| Cold | Lukewarm | Warm | Hot |
|---|---|---|---|
| SEO + LinkedIn posts (category education, founder thought leadership) | Case studies (real numbers, named ICPs) + Comparison pages (you vs. alternatives, bottom-funnel SEO) | Tailored Looms (1:1 videos referencing their stack + trigger) + Champion enablement (decks, business cases) | Proof docs (ROI calculators, security one-pagers) + Reference customers (intros with peers) |
| Podcast guesting (borrow other audiences) | | | |

#### Ads (Buy attention)

| Cold | Lukewarm | Warm | Hot |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn brand ads (thought leadership, ICP job titles) + Sponsorships (niche newsletters) | Retargeting (visitors get case studies, multi-touch proof) + Intent-based Google (category + competitor terms) | ABM account ads (target T1 by domain, all 5 committee roles) + Reach sequences (warm the whole buying team) | Air cover (keep brand visible during long cycles) + Proof ads to committee (customer logos, results) |

#### Cold Outreach (Get meetings)

| Cold | Lukewarm | Warm | Hot |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trigger-based DMs (only on triggered accounts, reference the event) + Engagement-first (comment → DM → ask) | Cold email sequences (multi-touch, SPICED-based, trigger is the opener) + LinkedIn outbound (personalised by role) | Multi-threaded outreach (champion + blocker + decision maker at once) + Cold call the committee (phone beats inbox here) | Stakeholder expansion (reach missed personas: CFO, Legal, IT) + Deal-saver outreach (when champion goes dark) |

#### Warm Content (Conversion)

| Cold | Lukewarm | Warm | Hot |
|---|---|---|---|
| Community presence (be where your ICP hangs: Slack groups, events) + Partner co-marketing (webinars, joint content) | Re-engage lapsed (old pipeline, past demos → new trigger = new reason) + Network intros (advisors, investors, peers) | Customer referrals (scripted, targeted T1 — best conversion rate) + Champion intros (from champions to peers) | Reference calls (peers talking to peers, closes deals faster) + Executive sponsor (founder-to-founder call) |

---

### Step 3 · Signals — Did It Land?

Intent you can measure. If these move, you're activating.

| Signal | What to measure |
|---|---|
| **Reply rate** | Cold + warm opens. Positive replies per 100 sent. |
| **Site behaviour** | Repeat visits. Pricing page views. Multi-person from same company. |
| **Committee depth** | How many roles engaging per target account. |
| **Meetings booked** | MQL to SQL per week. The only metric that pays. |

> North Star of TAM activation: **Meetings booked per week from target accounts**. Everything else is leading indicator.

---

### How to apply this framework

When a client or project needs B2B account activation:
1. **Map triggers first** — what business or demand signals indicate a buying window for this ICP?
2. **Classify account temperature** — cold (no awareness), lukewarm (aware, not engaged), warm (engaged, evaluating), hot (in active deal)
3. **Assign the right tactic per temperature** — don't send a reference call deck to a cold account; don't send category education to someone who's pricing
4. **Track the 4 signals** — reply rate, site behaviour, committee depth, meetings booked
5. **Iterate on triggers** — the opener that references the right trigger always outperforms generic outreach

## How to assist:

For **strategic tasks** (positioning, launch, channel selection):
1. **POV** — state your strategic recommendation clearly and directly
2. **Rationale** — explain the reasoning in 2-3 sentences max
3. **Action plan** — concrete next steps, prioritized
4. **Watch out for** — key risks or assumptions to validate

For **growth/execution tasks** (experiments, funnels, retention):
1. **Hypothesis** — "We believe that [action] will cause [metric] to improve because [reason]"
2. **Experiment design** — how to test it, what to measure, success criteria
3. **Expected impact** — order of magnitude estimate (directional, not precise)
4. **Priority** — high/medium/low based on effort vs. impact

When diagnosing growth problems:
- Always start with data: what do the numbers say?
- Identify the bottleneck in the funnel before proposing solutions
- Distinguish between acquisition, activation, and retention problems — the fix is completely different

Frameworks to apply when relevant:
- **AARRR**: Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Referral → Revenue
- **ICE scoring**: Impact × Confidence × Ease (1-10 each)
- **North Star + input metrics tree**
- **Cohort analysis**: new vs. retained vs. resurrected users
- **Payback period**: CAC / (ARPU × gross margin)

Always ask clarifying questions when context is missing: current metrics, biggest bottleneck, team size, budget, product stage, target audience, stage of company, ICP, current traction.
